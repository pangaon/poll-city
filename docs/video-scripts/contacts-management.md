# Video Walkthrough: Contact Management
Duration: ~4 minutes
Feature: CRM Contact Management (#3, #4)

## Script

### Scene 1: Opening (0:00 - 0:15)
SCREEN: Dashboard
VOICE: "Contact management is the core of Poll City. Every door you knock, every call you make, every supporter you identify — it all lives here. Let me walk you through it."

### Scene 2: Viewing Your Contacts (0:15 - 0:45)
SCREEN: Click "Contacts" in sidebar
VOICE: "Click Contacts in the sidebar. You will see every person in your campaign database. The table shows name, phone, email, support level, ward, and last contacted date."
ACTION: Point out the column headers
VOICE: "You can sort by any column — click the header. Hold Shift and click a second column for multi-sort. For example, sort by ward first, then by support level within each ward."
ACTION: Show the search bar
VOICE: "The search bar finds contacts by name, email, phone, or address. Start typing and results filter instantly."

### Scene 3: Filtering Contacts (0:45 - 1:15)
SCREEN: Show filter options
VOICE: "Click the filter icon to narrow your list. Filter by support level — show only undecided voters. Filter by ward — show only Ward 12. Filter by tags — show only volunteers. You can combine filters."
ACTION: Apply a support level filter
ACTION: Apply a ward filter
VOICE: "Save a filter preset so you can come back to it. Click 'Save Preset', give it a name like 'Undecided in Ward 12', and it appears in your presets dropdown."

### Scene 4: Adding a Contact (1:15 - 1:45)
SCREEN: Click "Add Contact" button
VOICE: "Click Add Contact. Fill in the basics: first name, last name, phone, email, address. Set their support level if you know it. Add them to a ward."
ACTION: Fill in the form fields
VOICE: "Hit Save. They are now in your database and will appear on walk lists, call lists, and GOTV priority lists."

### Scene 5: Contact Detail View (1:45 - 2:15)
SCREEN: Click on a contact name
VOICE: "Click any contact to see their full profile. You will see their complete information, interaction timeline, tasks, sign requests, and tags."
ACTION: Scroll through the timeline
VOICE: "The timeline shows every door knock, phone call, and note in chronological order. You can see exactly who spoke to this person and what they said."

### Scene 6: Editing a Contact (2:15 - 2:30)
SCREEN: Click Edit on a contact
VOICE: "Click Edit to update any field. Change their support level after a conversation. Add notes. Mark them as a volunteer. Every edit is logged in the audit trail."

### Scene 7: Bulk Actions (2:30 - 3:00)
SCREEN: Select multiple contacts using checkboxes
VOICE: "Select multiple contacts with the checkboxes. Then use Bulk Actions. You can update the support level for all of them at once — useful after a canvass shift where you identified 20 supporters."
ACTION: Select contacts, choose "Update Support Level", select "Supporter"
VOICE: "You can also bulk-tag contacts. Select your contacts, click Bulk Tag, choose a tag like 'Fundraiser Attendee'. Done."

### Scene 8: Custom Fields (3:00 - 3:20)
SCREEN: Show custom fields on a contact
VOICE: "Every campaign has different needs. Go to Settings, then Custom Fields. Create fields like 'Hydro Concern' or 'Lawn Sign Consent'. These appear on every contact card and are filterable."

### Scene 9: Exporting Contacts (3:20 - 3:40)
SCREEN: Click Export
VOICE: "Need a walk list for Saturday? Click Export, choose Walk List, filter by street or ward, and download a CSV. You can export contacts, GOTV lists, sign lists, or volunteer lists — all with custom filters."

### Scene 10: Closing (3:40 - 4:00)
SCREEN: Return to contacts list
VOICE: "That is contact management in Poll City. Import your voter list, knock doors, log results, and watch your support numbers grow. Every contact is a real person and a real conversation. Go to Import/Export to get your voter list in — that is the first step."
